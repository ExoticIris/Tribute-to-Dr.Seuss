Dr. Theodor Seuss Geisel

"Today you are you! That is truer than true! There is no one alive who is you-er than you!" ~from Dr. Seuss's Happy Birthday to You! (1959)

Dr. Seuss's Chronological Biography:

  • 1904 - Dr. Seuss was born in Springfield, Massachusetts. He was the son of German Immigrants Henrietta Seuss and Theodor Robert Geisel.
  • 1921 to 1925 - Theodor goes to Dartmouth College where he first took on his famous pen name "Seuss" to be able continue his work on the Jack-O-Lantern article without the administration's knowledge
    (with whom he had some conflict with).
  • 1926 - Theodor eventually adds the prefix "Dr." to his pen name to be officially called "Dr. Suess".
  • 1927 - Dr. Suess marries Helen Palmer whom he met Oxford University.
  • 1928 - Dr. Suess publishes his first artwork in for the Flit advertising campaign. The Flit was run for 17 years and made Dr. Seuss's tagline "Quick, Henry, the Flit!" a popular catchphrase in the United States.
  • 1931 - The Pocket Book of Boners is published as Seuss's first illustrated book.
  • 1937 - Dr. Suess and Robert Carington publish Seuss's first children's book And to Think I Saw It on Mulberry Street.
  • 1939 - World War II begins, and Seuss publishes The King's Stilts.
  • 1943 - Seuss joins the Army, where he served until 1946. He was the commander of the Animation Dept of the First Motion Picture Unit of the United States Army Air Forces.
  • 1947 - His book McElligot's Pool is published. The book is notable for starting with colored pages and ending with black and white pages.
  • 1954 - Horton Hears a Who is published, becoming a best seller and living on for decades.
  • 1957 - Two more of Seuss's biggest successes are published: The Cat in the Hat and How the Grinch Stole Christmas. He also becomes the president of his publishing company "Beginner Books" which he
    co-founded with Phyllis Cerf.
  • 1959 to 1960 - "Beginner Books" publishes more successful Suess hits including Happy Birthday to You!, Green Eggs and Ham, and One Fish Two Fish, Red Fish Blue Fish.
  • 1966 - How the Grinch Stole Christmas first airs on CBS as a 26-minute short American animated television special directed by Chuck Jones.
  • 1967 - Helen Palmer Geisel, Seuss's wife, commits suicide by overdosing.
  • 1968 - Theodor marries Audrey Stone Dimond.
  • 1971 - Seuss publishes The Lorax and is awarded He was awarded the Peabody Award for his television specials as well as the First Outstanding Author Award.
  • 1981 - In wake of his 77th birthday, Dr. Seuss birthday is declared globally as Dr. Seuss Day.
  • 1984 - A Pulitzer Prize is awarded to Dr. Seuss. Other prizes acquired by Seuss include an Academy Award, three Emmy Awards, three Grammy Awards, and three Caldecott Honors.
  • 1991 - Dr. Seuss passes away due to oral cancer at his home in La Jolla, leaving behind 44 written and illustrated books.
